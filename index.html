THE ARCHIVE

In the "Land of Saints and Scholars," the Fir-Dána were the Aes Dána—the people of skill.

They were the architects of tradition: the lawyers, the poets, and the historians who ensured that the great moments of life were anchored in something deeper than the individual. They didn't "facilitate" feelings; they constructed meaning.
